Part Selection

Motor:
Emax Eco 1404 High Efficiency Micro Motor (6000Kv)

This motor satisfies the requirements for a brushless DC (BLDC) motor that offers great performance at a cheap price.
A BLDC motor was ideal for the reaction wheels as brushed motors encounter arcing issues in the vacuum of space. This degrades performance and can destroy the motor.
Most commercial BLDC motors smaller than 4cm long are very expensive, £100+, and offer limited performance.
The disadvantages of using a commercial drone BLDC motor is the lack of encoder and the relatively high power draw. The latter can be dealt with by limiting the motor speed/current.
An encoder can be built into the flywheel of the reaction wheel, using either an optical or magnetic encoder.

+ Additional Part:
ESC:
DYS XSD BLHeli_S Dshot 300/60

This is a general purpose ESC for brushless motors, the 12A option is sufficient to drive an Emax Eco 1404 at 2S power. Most importantly it's incredibly compact and quite cheap.
It will be driven by pwm and due to its nature it can be replaced by similar, higher quality components in the future, if need be.

+ Additional Part:
Encoder: 
US5881 Unipolar Hall Switch
Low Sensitivity

Since each BLDC motor is rated at 6000Kv and powered using a 2S battery, 6000Kv * 7.4V = 44,400 rpm
44,400 rpm = 740Hz (4649.557122 rad/s)
In reality this is the no-load rotational speed and will be much lower when spinning a flywheel
Regardless the encoder should be able to measure a rotation roughly every 1ms (<0.5ms to detect change in state) 

A hall effect sensor and magnet has been chosen over an optical encoder as optical encoders may encounter issues with dust or particulate matter in space.
A traditional hall sensor is also much smaller than rotational magnetic encoders, allowing the whole reaction wheel unit to stay roughly the sum volume of the motor and flywheel.
Additionally, most magnetic rotary encoders feature a serial or i2c interface, which may take up valuable pins on the MCU. A simple hall switch requires only

The US5881 is a few millimeters wide and has a maximum switching frequency of 10kHz. The flywheel of each RW can then house a small neodymium magnet with the US5881 mounted to the RW enclosure.
